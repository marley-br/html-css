Jimi Hendrix playing at woodstock festival

Here's a time line of Jimi Hendrix career

  • 1961-1966: Hendrix played as a session musician and sideman for several R&B and soul acts, including Little Richard, Ike and Tina Turner, and the Isley Brothers.
  • 1966: Hendrix formed his own band, The Jimi Hendrix Experience, with bassist Noel Redding and drummer Mitch Mitchell.
  • 1967: The Jimi Hendrix Experience released their debut album, "Are You Experienced?", which became a critical and commercial success.
  • 1968: Hendrix released his second album with the Experience, "Axis: Bold as Love", and performed at the Monterey Pop Festival, where he famously set his guitar on fire.
  • 1969: Hendrix played at the Woodstock Festival and released his third and final album with the Experience, "Electric Ladyland".
  • 1970: Hendrix formed a new band, Band of Gypsys, and played a series of concerts at the Fillmore East in New York City. He also headlined the Isle of Wight Festival in England.
  • September 18, 1970: Hendrix passed away at the age of 27.

Despite his short career, Hendrix had a profound influence on rock music and is widely considered one of the greatest guitarists of all time.
